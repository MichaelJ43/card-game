# Durak (two-player simplified)

Attack/defend practice on a 36-card deck (6–A). Rules are shortened; this is not a full multi-player Durak table.

## Goal

1. When the **stock is empty**, the first player to **empty their hand** wins the round.
2. The other player is the “durak” (fool) for scoring purposes in this app: the round winner earns the match point.

## Trump

1. After six cards are dealt to each player, the next card is turned face up as **trump** and is out of play.
2. Any trump beats any non-trump card.
3. On non-trump attacks, the defender may beat with a **higher card of the same suit** or with **any trump**.

## Attack and defense

1. The **attacker** plays one card face up to the battle area.
2. The **defender** either:
   1. Plays a single card that **beats** the attack card (both cards are discarded to waste), then roles swap and the successful defender becomes the next attacker; or
   2. **Takes** the attack card into hand; the same attacker attacks again.
3. After taking or finishing a successful defense, both players **refill toward six cards** from stock if possible (attacker draws first in this build).

## Rank order

1. Low to high: 6, 7, 8, 9, 10, J, Q, K, A.

## Notes

1. Configurable AI opponent count applies like other two-player tables (default one AI).
2. The status line shows whether the active seat is attacking or defending.
